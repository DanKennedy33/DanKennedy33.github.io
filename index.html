My Work Placement

Bank of Ireland, IT Centre Cabinteely

In third year of the IT Management Course in IT Tallaght, students are required to undertake in work experience in some area of the IT industry. I applied for a number of different jobs that were posted on the work placement moodle page and eventually obtained work placement with Bank of Ireland in their IT Centre in Cabinteely. I was informed that I was going to be working with the Service Governance and Reporting team within the overall Service Control Team. Within this team I would move around working with each of the 15 members during my 6 months on placement.

I began my placement in January with the Access Control Team within User Access Management. This team had three members, one looking after Forefront Identity Manager (FIM), one provisioning access to Global Market applications and one working with Varonis. Within this team, I was performing simple tasks such as resetting passwords, granting access to Global Markets Applications and removing access from people who have left the bank through FIM. I enjoyed working with this team and the other members really helped me settle in and get used to working life within Bank Of Ireland. However as I got used to the different tasks I would be completing, they became repetitive and I also found that there was not enough work to delegate between me and the three other staff that were working within the Access Control Team.

In April, I moved to the Service Reporting team with one other member, my Work Placement Manager, Damien Purcell. Within this team my main job was to obtain performance reports from IBM and other vendors and enter them into our ITSM tool ISM. I also was in charge of monitoring the daily governance interlock to obtain the status of different events effecting reporting. I with this team for less than a month when I was informed that I was required to fill a vacancy under the User Access & Recertification manager, Kevin McDonough, for the upcoming Recertification Cycle.

I spent the rest of my time with Bank of Ireland working with both Kevin and IT Central Content Manager, Beverley Thompson. I would delegate my time between Kevin, helping him with the Recertification of all the applications in the bank and assisting Beverley with the maintenance of IT Central, which is the bank's internal site for requesting IT Services (creating tickets) and Equipment.I was in charge of preparing Application Data Extracts for Recertification and passing them onto the IBM team who would put them through a matching tool in order to put them on a sharepoint site. On the sharepoint site each of the line managers could then complete their actions, by either removing access to their staff or granting the current access.

I have gained a lot of experience working with Bank of Ireland and it opened my eyes to an area of IT that I was unfamiliar with. I ended up staying on with the bank after my work experience working with the User Access Management team until the end of November, assisting with the second Recertification Cycle and then training in the new employee hired to fill the vacancy that I was leaving. I have been informed that there is potential for me to obtain a job with the Bank upon completion of my college course.

Industry Project

Review of company and practical element

During my third year work placement we were tasked with completing an Idustry Project containing a Host Company Review, a detailed description of work undertaken on placement and also our Mini Project. The Mini Project was the main part of the Industry Project and required the student to complete practical work on something to do with their workplace.

I chose to complete my Mini Project based on something that was actively happening within the Bank, particularly within the Technology Services area, which I was working in. The title of my project was a "Critical Evaluation of the benefits and drawbacks of Bank of Ireland’s transition from IBM ISM service management tool to a ServiceNow toolset". It contains a detailed review of the benefits and disadvantages of Bank of Ireland's proposed migration from IBM's ISM toolset to ServiceNow's, industry leading ITSM toolset.
